Baga beach

Baga Beach in Goa is one of the most popular beaches in the area, and it's easy to see why. This beach has something for everyone. If you're in the mood for adventure, you can try exciting water sports like parasailing, jet-skiing, or even windsurfing.

The beach is lined with shacks where you can enjoy tasty Goan food and sip on a cool drink while watching the waves. As the sun sets, Baga Beach comes alive with music and dancing, especially at famous nightclubs like Tito's and Mambo's. Whether you want to relax on the sand during the day or party at night, Baga Beach is the place to be.

Anjuna beach

Anjuna Beach is known for its relaxed, bohemian vibe, making it a favorite among those looking to unwind. The beach features stunning red cliffs that offer a picturesque backdrop to the golden sands

On Wednesdays, the lively Anjuna Flea Market draws visitors with its handmade jewelry, clothing, and souvenirs. Whether you're exploring the market or enjoying a quiet meal at a beachside café, Anjuna Beach offers a perfect blend of nature and relaxation.

Goa port

Perched on a hill overlooking the Arabian Sea, Fort Aguada is one of Goa's iconic landmarks. Built by the Portuguese in the 17th century, this massive fort served as a crucial defense against invaders, guarding the coastline and the mouth of the Mandovi.

The fort is well-preserved and offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape. One of its most distinctive features is the four-story lighthouse, which was once the tallest in Asia. A visit to Fort Aguada is a step back in time, offering a glimpse into Goa's rich history while also providing a perfect spot to watch the sunset.
